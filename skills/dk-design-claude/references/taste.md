# Taste kernel

Read only during Explore, Reimagine, or material visual uncertainty.

## Start from the job

Use real content and decide the first, second, and third focal points before decoration. A brand surface may spend more expression on story and recall; a product surface must protect repeated tasks, data density, states, and speed. Accessibility and the user task outrank visual convention.

## Make alternatives informative

Hold content, function, and viewport constant. Three useful concepts differ in composition, type roles, density, geometry, color relationship, and one signature. Recolors are one concept. Show enough of the complete surface to judge hierarchy and character, not isolated mood fragments.

Choose one honest tension instead of a pile of safe adjectives: calm but decisive, technical but humane, dense but legible. Give the product one repeatable signature that helps meaning or navigation. If removing the logo makes the result interchangeable with a category template, the direction is not finished.

## Build a small system

Extract only durable rules: composition, responsive priority, typography, color budget, form, motion/media stance, anti-goals, and semantic bindings. Keep machine-enforced values in the token SSOT. Keep the short human rationale in `direction.json`. Do not create a second DESIGN.md or restate the entire component library in prompts.

## Learn without cloning

From references, extract relationships rather than expression: hierarchy, density, rhythm, type contrast, color allocation, interaction logic, and why they fit the task. Record provenance outside the core contract under `.dk/dk-design/`. Do not copy proprietary prompts, code, copy, assets, pixel layouts, or a living designer's signature style. If a source cannot be inspected, do not invent what it contains.

## Judge the pixels

Compare accepted intent with the rendered surface, not with source-code confidence. Prioritize structural mismatch before detail polish. Fix at most three material deltas per pass; repeated unconstrained polishing usually erodes identity.
